## Oxalis acetosella — Wood Sorrel

**Common Name:** Wood Sorrel
**Botanical Name:** Oxalis acetosella
**Family:** Oxalidaceae

### Identifying Features
- Heart-shaped leaves in groups of three (like a clover)
- Leaves fold down at night or in bright sun
- Small white flowers with pink/lilac veins
- Low growing, found in shaded woodland

### Habitat
Where it's typically found, what it grows near

### Edibility & Use
- Edible raw — lemony sharp taste
- Contains oxalic acid — eat in small quantities
- Good as a trail nibble, can be added to salads

### Lookalikes & Risks
- Often confused with clover — clover has rounded not heart shaped leaves, clover is also not poisonous. 
- High in oxalic acid — not suitable in large quantities for people with kidney issues

## Foraging Safety Reference

### Things eaten by everyone with no known reactions
- Dandelion leaves
- Dock leaves
- Ground Elder areal plant
- Hedge Garlic leaves
- Wild Garlic leaves
- Dog Rose Hips
- Stinging Nettles (new growth only)
- Hedge Woundwort

---

### Plants tried with caution as informed choice — not to be included in the main meal, may be served as additional side dish to accompany meal
- **Cleavers** — Can cause skin irritation to sensitive skin when handled
- **Oxeye Daisy** — Can cause contact dermatitis
- **Hawthorn** — Avoid all if on heart medication. Amygdalin in stone — body saliva enzymes free the cyanide compound. 10 min heat destroys it
- **Hogweed** — Can increase UV sensitivity, can make some a little nauseous
- **Yarrow** — Can cause skin irritation when handling. Contains Thujone — toxic if consumed in quantity. Unlikely to be issue if consumed as food. Avoid if pregnant as a precaution
- **Mugwort** — Contains small amounts of Thujone. Toxic if consumed in quantity, avoid if pregnant or breastfeeding
- **Colts Foot** — Avoid if pregnant/breastfeeding/child. Contains small amounts Pyrrolizidine Alkaloid — can cause liver damage if eaten over long periods
- **Primrose** — Not high doses if pregnant as uterine stimulant, can cause contact dermatitis. Contains Salacin — avoid if allergic to aspirin or on warfarine

---

### Plants on forage with cautions — may be toxic
- **Stinging Nettles** — New growth only. Calcium carbonate in leaves after flowering — liver damage. Advise avoid root if pregnant
- **Mallow** — Purgative and laxative if too much
- **Horse Chestnut** — High in saponins which have haemolytic properties — highly toxic if entire blood stream
- **Hawthorn** — Avoid all if on heart medication. Amygdalin in stone — body saliva enzymes free the cyanide compound. 10 min heat destroys it
- **Black Bryony** — All plant is poisonous (climber, red berries mix in with others — care when collecting edible berries)
- **Lords and Ladies** — Poisonous, calcium oxalate crystals. When very young can be mistaken for Common Sorrel
- **English Oak** — Tannins carcinogenic over long periods
- **Yew** — Contains Taxine, various alkaloids, irritating volatile oils and glycoside. All parts fatal in small amounts (only the flesh on the berries is edible, seeds inside highly toxic)
- **Goat Willow** — Avoid if aspirin allergy or on wolverine medication or pregnant
- **Dogs Mercury** — Poisonous, leaves have been mistaken for peppermint and ground elder
- **Elder** — Care when collecting, leaves can look like Dogs Mercury. Both plants can grow together. Leaves and twigs contain toxic levels of cyanogenic glycosides, berries at lower levels
- **Holly** — Berries are poisonous
- **Hemlock** — Contains coniine in small amounts — causes muscular paralysis and death
- **Yarrow** — Can cause skin irritation when handling. Contains Thujone — toxic if consumed in quantity. Unlikely to be issue if consumed as food. Avoid if pregnant as a precaution
- **Clematis** — Contains protoanemonin, breaks down with cooking
- **Hogweed** — Phototoxic, increases photosensitivity and/or makes some a little nauseous

---

### Additional cautions
- **Common Sorrel** — High in oxalic acid, eat in moderation and avoid if have kidney stones. Can be mistaken for very young Lords and Ladies
- **Mugwort** — Contains small amounts of Thujone. Toxic if consumed in quantity, avoid if pregnant or breastfeeding
- **Colts Foot** — Avoid if pregnant/breastfeeding/child. Contains small amounts Pyrrolizidine Alkaloid — cause liver damage if eaten over long periods
- **Comfrey** — Pyrrolizidine Alkaloid and Carcinogen Symphytum — cause liver damage and benign tumour in animal tests. Avoid if pregnant (including child)
- **Blackthorne** — Risk of infection from the combination of thorns and bacteria on the bark
- **Dog Rose** — Rose hips contain irritant hairs, strain through muslin cloth to remove
- **Pendula Sedge** — Silica, possibly irritate liver (worse if already inflamed)
- **Lesser Celandine** — Contains Protoanemonin in minute quantities
- **Wood Sorrel** — In moderation, avoid if have kidney stones. Contains oxalic acid — in quantity forms insoluble salts binding to calcium to form kidney stones
- **Vetch** — Contains amino acids, cyanogens and other toxins. Causes lathyrism in large quantities and not boiled for 2 hours
- **Chickweed** — Mildly toxic in huge quantities due to saponins. Caution — farmers spray herbicides in pastures where it is found before collecting, to prevent poisoning livestock
- **Honeysuckle** — Saponins in red berries make it poisonous if eaten in large amounts
- **Red Campion** — Not edible, high in saponins
- **Beech** — Risk of large branches suddenly dropping
