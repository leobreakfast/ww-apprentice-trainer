## Oxalis acetosella — Wood Sorrel

**Common Name:** Wood Sorrel
**Botanical Name:** Oxalis acetosella
**Family:** Oxalidaceae

### Identifying Features
- Heart-shaped leaves in groups of three (like a clover)
- Leaves fold down at night or in bright sun
- Small white flowers with pink/lilac veins
- Low growing, found in shaded woodland

### Habitat
Where it's typically found, what it grows near

### Edibility & Use
- Edible raw — lemony sharp taste
- Contains oxalic acid — eat in small quantities
- Good as a trail nibble, can be added to salads

### Lookalikes & Risks
- Often confused with clover — clover has rounded not heart shaped leaves, clover is also not poisonous. 
- High in oxalic acid — not suitable in large quantities for people with kidney issues
