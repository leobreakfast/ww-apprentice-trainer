### Intro Wood Pigeon
    - Take pigeon out of box
    - Introduce Columba palumbus (wood pigeon)
    - Say it has been lawfully sourced
    - Although dead, we request that customers treat them respectfully
    - Identification
        - white spots on collar
        - white lines down edge of wing feathers
        - black bar on end of tail feather
        - white creamy breast
    - Compare to Columba livia domestica (domestic rock dove)
        - Street pigeons are ferral rock doves
        - Interesting fact (only mention if pushed)
            - A long time ago humans domesticated rock doves, as they were easy to capture and train
            - early 1700's they were named Columba domestica (Domestic Pigeon)
            - late 1700's Rock Doves were named Columba livia 
            - later they realised they were the same species, and renamed to Columba livia domestica

    - Found on farmland, woodland edges parks, hedgerows etc
    - Nests are untidy and made of twigs
    - Usually lay 2 eggs which hatch after around 18 days
    - Many die around 3 years, can live several years if lucky
    - Around 5 million breeding pairs
        - around 2.2 million breading pairs in 1960s
        - numbers rose strongly suspected due to modern agricultture and winter crops    
    - Now there are so many they are culled to protect crops
    - Mostly shot
    - Historically they have been trapped
    - The law 
        - it is illegal to trap or kill a wild animal in the uk, wildlife and countryside act 1981
        - exceptions are made for wood pigeons if for culling
        - however traps are only allowed if they are specific designs that have been approved 
    - Put pigeon back in box
    - introduce cage trap
    - Take pigeon out of box
    - explain humane dispatch
        - two fingers around neck gently but firmly, spin arm to twist and break neck
        - two fingers round neck hold firmly with other hand, twist in opposite directions
        - hit over head
    
    - move on to preperation
