# Woodland Ways Apprentice Trainer — Planning Doc

## Purpose
A retrieval-based training system that turns instructors from 
"recognising the right answer" into confidently recalling, 
explaining, and delivering every part of the bushcraft syllabus.

## Progression System
- Spark — Recognition (multiple choice)
- Ember — Guided Recall (fill in the blank)
- Flame — Free Recall (full typed answers, spellings graded)

## Confidence Rating
After every answer the user rates themselves:
- Low / Medium / High
- High confidence + wrong = urgent review
- Low confidence + correct = reinforce soon

## Spaced Repetition
- Correct answer = delayed reappearance
- Wrong answer = rapid repetition
- Weak answers = re-tested soon

## First Module — Foraging
Starting with 3 plants:
- Stellaria media (Chickweed)
- Glechoma hederacea (Ground Ivy)
- Oxalis acetosella (Wood Sorrel)

## Content Plan
- Build Foraging module fully first
- Add plants gradually over time
- Once Foraging is solid, move to next module

## Question Flow (Scene-Based)

Each plant is experienced as a scene, not a list of questions.
The instructor encounters the plant as they would in a real session.

Example flow for Oxalis acetosella:

1. Photo of plant shown
   "You spot something low to the ground near a fallen oak..."
   → What plant is this? (multiple choice at Spark)

2. "A customer asks what it's called scientifically..."
   → What is the botanical name? (typed at Flame)

3. "A customer asks how they can be sure it's safe..."
   → Name two identifying features (typed at Flame)

4. Confidence rating after each answer (Low / Medium / High)

## Why Scene-Based?
- Simulates real pressure of a live session
- Tests recall in context not just isolated facts
- More engaging than a flat quiz
- Mirrors how knowledge is actually used

## Question Order Per Plant (Spark level example)
1. Plant identification from photo
2. Common name
3. Botanical name (recognition at Spark, recall at Flame)
4. Two identifying features
5. Edibility / use
6. What would you say to a customer?
7. Any lookalikes or risks
